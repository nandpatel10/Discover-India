About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World, is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna River in Agra, India. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

History

The construction of the Taj Mahal took approximately 22 years and involved around 20,000 artisans. The architectural beauty, intricate carvings, and the stunning symmetry make it one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture.

Architecture

The Taj Mahal is a perfect blend of Persian, Turkish, and Indian architectural styles. It features a large central dome, minarets, gardens, and intricate marble inlays.

Tourism

Millions of tourists visit the Taj Mahal every year to witness its breathtaking beauty. The best time to visit is during sunrise or sunset when the monument appears to change colors.
