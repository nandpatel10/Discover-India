About Kerala

History of Kerala

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," has a history dating back thousands of years. It was a major spice trade center, attracting merchants from Rome, Greece, China, and the Middle East. The Chera dynasty ruled Kerala in ancient times, followed by colonial influences from the Portuguese, Dutch, and British. Kerala played a significant role in India's independence movement and is now a state known for its literacy, healthcare, and unique culture.

Major Attractions

Kerala is famous for its serene backwaters, hill stations, pristine beaches, and rich cultural heritage. Must-visit places include:

  • Alleppey Backwaters: Famous for houseboat cruises and scenic landscapes.
  • Munnar: A picturesque hill station known for tea plantations and cool climate.
  • Kochi: A historic port city with colonial-era architecture and Chinese fishing nets.
  • Kovalam Beach: A popular beach with golden sands and Ayurveda resorts.
  • Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary: Home to elephants, tigers, and diverse flora and fauna.
  • Varkala Beach: Known for its red cliffs, temples, and laid-back atmosphere.

Culture and Festivals

Kerala's culture is deeply rooted in traditions, art forms, and festivals. Some major festivals include:

  • Onam: The harvest festival celebrated with feasts, boat races, and traditional dances.
  • Vishu: Kerala's New Year festival featuring fireworks and special meals.
  • Theyyam: A ritual dance-drama performed in temples.
  • Thrissur Pooram: A grand temple festival featuring elephant parades and fireworks.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kerala is from September to March, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for exploring beaches, backwaters, and hill stations. The monsoon season (June to August) is perfect for Ayurvedic treatments and lush green landscapes.

Local Cuisine

Kerala's cuisine is known for its use of coconut, seafood, and spices. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Sadya: A traditional feast served on a banana leaf, consisting of rice, curries, and desserts.
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry: Steamed rice cakes with black chickpea curry.
  • Appam with Stew: Soft rice pancakes paired with coconut-based vegetable or meat stew.
  • Karimeen Pollichathu: A delicious dish of pearl spot fish marinated and grilled in banana leaves.
  • Payasam: A sweet pudding made with rice, jaggery, and coconut milk.
