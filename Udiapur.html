About Udaipur

History of Udaipur

Udaipur, often called the "City of Lakes," was founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II, the ruler of Mewar. The city was established as the new capital of Mewar after Chittorgarh was repeatedly attacked by the Mughals. Surrounded by the Aravalli Hills and dotted with stunning lakes, Udaipur became a strategic and picturesque stronghold. It remained a princely state until Indiaâ€™s independence in 1947 and continues to captivate visitors with its regal charm and historical significance.

Major Attractions

Udaipur boasts a blend of royal architecture, scenic lakes, and cultural heritage. Some must-visit places include:

  • City Palace: A grand palace complex showcasing Rajput architecture and offering stunning views of Lake Pichola.
  • Lake Pichola: An artificial freshwater lake with picturesque boat rides and stunning views of City Palace and Jag Mandir.
  • Jag Mandir: A beautiful island palace in Lake Pichola, often referred to as the "Lake Garden Palace."
  • Fateh Sagar Lake: Another serene lake with boat rides and Nehru Park in the middle.
  • Saheliyon Ki Bari: A historic garden built for the royal women, featuring fountains, lotus pools, and marble pavilions.
  • Jagdish Temple: A large and artistically significant temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Festivals and Celebrations

Udaipur is known for its grand celebrations of traditional Rajasthani festivals. Some major ones include:

  • Mewar Festival: A vibrant festival celebrating spring with cultural performances, processions, and traditional music.
  • Gangaur Festival: Dedicated to Goddess Gauri, this festival features processions of beautifully adorned idols.
  • Diwali: The city is beautifully lit up with lamps and fireworks to mark the festival of lights.
  • Shilpgram Festival: An annual arts and crafts fair promoting traditional Rajasthani culture.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Udaipur is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant for sightseeing and outdoor activities. The monsoon season (July to September) also adds charm to the city's lakes and greenery.

Local Cuisine

Udaipur offers a rich variety of traditional Rajasthani dishes. Some must-try foods include:

  • Dal Baati Churma: A signature Rajasthani dish consisting of baked wheat dumplings served with spiced lentils and sweet churma.
  • Gatte ki Sabzi: A delicious curry made with gram flour dumplings cooked in spicy yogurt gravy.
  • Laal Maas: A traditional spicy mutton curry known for its rich flavors.
  • Mirchi Bada: A deep-fried spicy chili snack, perfect for street food lovers.
  • Mawa Kachori: A sweet pastry stuffed with rich mawa (khoya) and dry fruits.
