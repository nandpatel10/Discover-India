Festivals of India

India celebrates a variety of festivals throughout the year, each reflecting the country's rich cultural heritage and religious diversity.

Diwali

The Festival of Lights, symbolizing the victory of light over darkness.

Holi

The Festival of Colors, celebrated with vibrant powders and joy.

Eid-ul-Fitr

Marking the end of Ramadan with feasts and prayers.

Christmas

Celebrating the birth of Jesus Christ with joy and gifts.

Navratri

A nine-day festival honoring Goddess Durga with dance and devotion.

Pongal

A Tamil harvest festival thanking the Sun God for prosperity.

Bihu

The Assamese harvest festival celebrating the agricultural cycle.

Lohri

A Punjabi winter festival marking the harvest of sugarcane.

Onam

Kerala's harvest festival celebrated with grand feasts and boat races.

Raksha Bandhan

A festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters.

Ganesh Chaturthi

A festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha with grand processions.

Mahsavriati

A festival celebrating the birth of Lord Shiva with grand processions.

Religions of India

India is home to many religions such as Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, and Jainism, each with its unique traditions and beliefs.

Culture by States

Each Indian state has its own rich heritage, from the dance forms of Kerala to the vibrant traditions of Rajasthan.
